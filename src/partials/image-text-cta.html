We all know that Earth’s population is growing. But do you know how fast? According to the Pew Research Center we’ll go from 7 billion to 9.6 billion by 2050. That means we’re going to have a lot of extra mouths to feed.


So how are we going to adapt? Likely it’s going to be a group effort. One thing we know for sure, though, is that agricultural operations of all shapes and sizes are going to be a critical part of the equation.


Which got us thinking:

is there anything we can do to help?


Our minirhizotron systems enable agricultural researchers to:

  • Capture non-destructive, high resolution, digital images to observe the development and function of a plant’s root system.
  • Analyze root images to learn important information about the root health and development of their crops.
  • Track established and newly rooted crops throughout the growing season non-destructively.
  • Diagnose plant pathogens and disorders by acting as an early detection system.
